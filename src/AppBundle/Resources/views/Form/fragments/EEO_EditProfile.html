Kirkland & Ellis LLP values and appreciates individuals from diverse social, economic, cultural, and personal backgrounds. All employment decisions, including, but not limited to, recruiting, hiring, placement, promotion, compensation, and evaluation are made without regard to the employee's sex, race, color, gender, religion, national origin, citizenship, pregnancy, age, marital status, ancestry, physical or mental disability, medical condition, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, genetic information, or any other characteristic protected under federal, state, or local law.

Kirkland & Ellis LLP is subject to certain governmental record-keeping and reporting requirements for administration of civil rights laws and regulations. In order to comply with these requirements, and in order to maintain statistics used to measure the Firm's success in building and maintaining a diverse workforce, the Firm invites individuals to voluntarily self-identify their gender, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, gender identity, gender expression, and other personal characteristics as indicated below. Submission of the requested information is voluntary and refusal to provide any will not subject you to any adverse treatment.
