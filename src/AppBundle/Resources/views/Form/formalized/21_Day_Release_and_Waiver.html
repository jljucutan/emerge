Intending to be legally bound, Employee and Company agree as follows:

The severance payment will be paid within ten (10) business days following the later of the expiration of the seven (7) day revocation period following employee’s timely execution of this Release Agreement, and Employee’s return of the Company information and property.
2. IN EXCHANGE for the consideration referenced in Section 1 of this Release, Employee hereby, on behalf of Employee and Employee’s heirs, executors, administrators, assigns and successors, fully and forever releases and discharges, to the maximum extent permitted by law, Company and its parent, subsidiaries, affiliates, divisions, successors, and assigns, together with their and the Company’s past and present directors, officers, agents, attorneys, insurers, employees, stockholders, benefit plans, and any person/entity claimed to be jointly or severally liable with the Company with respect to any legal liability (collectively “Released Parties”), from any and all claims, causes of action, complaints, lawsuits and liabilities of any kind, whether now known or unknown, suspected or unsuspected, accrued or unaccrued, which Employee has or may claim to have against Company or any of the other Released Parties, arising at any time up to and including the date that Employee signs this Release (“Claims”). The foregoing release covers, includes, and therefore releases any and all Claims arising out of or in any way connected with Employee's employment with Company or the termination of that employment, including (but not limited to):
  1. any Claims having anything to do with Employee’s employment with the Company and/or any other Released Party;
  2. any Claims having anything to do with the termination of Employee’s employment with the Company and/or any other Released Party;
  3. any Claims for unpaid or withhold wages, severance, benefits, bonuses, commissions and/or compensation of any kind, including but not limited to claims under the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938, as amended, or similar federal, state or local laws;
  4. any claims for attorneys’ fees or costs;
  5. any Claims arising under any law prohibiting discrimination and/or harassment on the basis of any protected characteristic (including but not limited to age, race, color, creed, sex, national origin, disability status, handicap, citizenship, ancestry, religion, and sexual orientation), including (but not limited to) the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (“ADEA”), 29 U.S.C. Section 621 et. seq., Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, as amended, Section 1981 of the Civil Rights Act of 1866, the Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act, the Equal Pay Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, any similar or equivalent employee-protective federal, state or local laws and any Claims for retaliation under any of the foregoing laws;
  6. any claims regarding leaves of absence including but not limited to any Claims under the Family and Medical Leave Act and/or any other federal, state or local leave related law;
  7. any Claims relating to the provision of employee benefits, including (but not limited to) the Employee Retirement Income Security Act, as amended (except for any vested benefits under any tax qualified benefit plan);
  8. any Claims under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 or similar federal, state and local laws;
  9. any whistleblower and/or retaliation Claims;
  10. any Claims arising under the common law of any jurisdiction, including (but not limited) any Claims for wrongful discharge, breach of contract, infliction of emotional distress, pain and suffering and defamation; and
  11. any Claims arising under any law, whether federal, state or local, governing the employment relationship.
If any claim is not subject to release, to the extent permitted by law, Employee also waives any right or ability to be a class or collective action representative or to otherwise participate in any putative or certified class or collective or multi-party action or proceeding based on such a claim in which any Released Party is a party.
3. The release in the previous paragraph shall not, however, release any rights to (i) those items to be paid as consideration under the Release Agreement, (ii) payments to which Employee is entitled under any Company insurance, retirement, profit sharing, deferred compensation, 401(k), stock purchase or any other Company benefit plan as of the Separation Date – the benefits under which will be paid in accordance with the terms of such plans, it being understood and agreed that neither the consideration paid under this Release Agreement nor the way that consideration is calculated or paid shall create or enhance any such entitlement, (iii) exercise any stock options/SARs that are vested as of the Separation Date at any time for two weeks following the Separation Date, or (iv) payment of salary through the Separation Date, all unused Paid Days Off earned and accrued through the Separation Date and reimbursement of any covered business expenses incurred by Employee in connection with the Company’s business and in accordance with the Company’s policy for the reimbursement of such expenses.
4. Employee warrants and agrees that Employee is responsible for any federal, state, and local taxes which may be owed by Employee by virtue of the receipt of any portion of the consideration herein provided. Company will, however, make any appropriate withholdings on amounts to be paid hereunder, as required by law.
5. Employee agrees not to publish, disclose, use or authorize anyone to publish, disclose or use without written authorization by Company any private, confidential, or proprietary information that Employee may have in any way acquired, learned, developed, or created by reason of Employee’s employment. In addition to information described in any employment agreement that addresses the use or disclosure of Company’s confidential information, such information includes the Company’s governance, finances, operations, plans, strategies, and organization, litigation and negotiations, marketing information, market research data; financial information, debt arrangements, equity structures, investors and holdings; operational and technical information, personnel information, sales, sales volume, sales methods, sales proposals, sales plans, pricing policies, fees, strategies, methods, specifications, and preferences of customers and potential customers, terms of customer contracts and/or agreements, the Company's computer programs, designs, software and other technology, system documentation, special hardware, manuals, formulae, products (whether existing, in development, being contemplated, or those the Company developed, tested, and decided not to market), processes, methods, equipment, machines, compositions, ideas, improvements, inventions, other confidential or proprietary information belonging to the Company or relating to the Company's affairs, and all similar information from, belonging to, or relating to Company-related entities, and other third parties. Nothing herein shall restrict Employee from using Employee’s general knowledge, skill, and experience with the Company.
6. Employee agrees to return immediately to Company all Company property and documents that Employee may have in Employee’s possession.
7. In the case in which the Company had provided Employee with a company credit card in connection with Employee’s employment with Company, Employee agrees to pay all sums due and owing from Employee in connection with such company credit card, including interest charges, if any. Additionally, Employee hereby authorizes Company to withhold from payments otherwise due to Employee hereunder an amount sufficient to pay all such sums due and owing from Employee in connection with such Company credit card.
8. Employee agrees not to use any computer or network access code or password belonging to Company or made available to Employee by virtue of Employee’s employment with Company, and not to access any computer, network, or database in the possession or control of Company.
9. Employee agrees not to make any negative comments or disparaging remarks, in writing, orally or electronically about the Company, any other Released Party or the services of any Released Party. However, nothing in this Release Agreement shall be interpreted to restrict Employee’s right and obligation to testify truthfully in any forum and/or to contact, cooperate with or provide information to any government agency or commission.
10. Employee agrees to cooperate with the Company and its attorneys in the prosecution and/or defense of any legal action wherein the Company is a party and that involves any facts or circumstances of which the Employee has knowledge due to Employee’s employment with the Company. Such cooperation includes, but is not limited to, meeting with the Company’s attorneys at reasonable times and places to discuss truthfully your knowledge of pertinent facts, appearing as required at deposition, arbitration, trial or other proceeding to testify as to those facts, and testifying truthfully to the best of your abilities at any such proceeding.
11. Company is under no obligation to consider Employee for reinstatement, rehire, future employment or any employment relationship with the Company and the Employee waives any rights Employee may have with respect to the Company’s consideration regarding any employment relationship with the Company. The Company is entitled to reject without cause any application for employment made by Employee and this Release Agreement will constitute a legitimate, non-discriminatory, non-retaliatory reason for terminating such employment.
12. Employee agrees to maintain the terms of this Release Agreement as confidential and not to disclose such terms to any party except Employee’s immediate family and, for purposes of obtaining professional advice only, Employee’s legal and financial advisors.
13. This Release Agreement constitutes the entire agreement concerning the termination of Employee's employment and all other subjects addressed herein. This Release Agreement supersedes and replaces all prior negotiations and agreements concerning its subject matter. Notwithstanding the above, any post-termination obligations, including but not limited to obligations with respect to confidentiality of the Company’s information and prohibitions against competing with, or soliciting the customers and/or employees of the Company that the Employee assumed in the Employee’s Employment Agreement (see attached for reference) or in any other agreement with the Company shall continue to apply and are incorporated by reference into this Release Agreement.
14. If one or more of the provisions of this Release shall for any reason be held invalid, illegal or unenforceable in any respect, such invalidity, illegality or unenforceability shall not affect or impair any other provision of this Release, but this Release shall be construed as if such invalid, illegal or unenforceable provision had not been contained herein.
15. This Release Agreement shall be interpreted and enforced according to the laws of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania without regard to the principles of conflicts of law otherwise applicable therein.
16. By signing below, Employee hereby acknowledges and agrees that Employee has carefully read and fully understands all of the provisions and effects of this Release Agreement; has been advised to consult and discuss all aspects of this Release Agreement with an attorney; has been given twenty-one (21) days within which to consider the Release Agreement; is voluntarily and knowingly entering into this Release Agreement free of coercion and duress; that neither Company nor any of its agents or attorneys has made any representations or promises concerning the terms or effects of this Release Agreement and Employee has adequate information to make a knowing and voluntary waiver.
17. If Employee signs this Release Agreement, Employee retains the right to revoke it for seven (7) days. The Release Agreement will not be effective until after the seven (7) day revocation period has expired without Employee having revoked Employee’s signature and the Release Agreement, The letter must be post-marked within seven (7) days of Employee’s execution of this Release Agreement and sent to CDI Corporation, Human Resources Department, 11200 Richmond Ave., Suite 500, Houston, TX 77082 If Employee revokes the Release Agreement on a timely basis, Employee will not be eligible for the payments set forth in Section 1 above.
