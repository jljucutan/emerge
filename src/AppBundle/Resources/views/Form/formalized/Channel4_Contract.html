SALARY INFORMATION
Money – getting paid

You will be paid monthly by direct transfer to your bank or building society account on or around the 15th of each month. This means that two weeks are paid in advance and two weeks are paid in arrears.

Remember to let us know as soon as you can if you change your bank account details so we can ensure your salary is paid into the right account.

Tax administration and expenses

We will deduct income tax and National Insurance from your salary.

The Corporation may (including on termination of your employment) deduct any sums from time to time owed by you to the Corporation for whatever reason from any payment due to you from the Corporation.

You are responsible for your own personal tax affairs and for ensuring that you are paying tax at the correct rate. For this reason it is especially important that you provide a P45 or HMRC New Joiner Checklist on starting employment and check your payslips on a regular basis.

We will deduct tax based on your P45 or other instruction from the HMRC. If you think your tax code is wrong then it is your responsibility to resolve this with HMRC.

Salary review

Salaries are normally reviewed annually but there is no guarantee of an increase in salary.

GENERAL EMPLOYMENT MATTERS
Place of work

You will initially be based at the location given on the summary chart on page 1. If you are based in London we reserve the right to relocate your employment to anywhere in the Greater London area (without additional remuneration), should business circumstances require this.

You agree to travel on the Corporation's business (either within the United Kingdom or abroad) as may be required for the proper performance of your duties.

Terms of Employment

Your probationary period is referenced within the covering table. Your employment may be terminated during this period at any time on one week's notice or payment in lieu of notice. The Corporation may, at its discretion, extend the probationary period for up to a further 3 months. During the probationary period your performance and suitability for continued employment will be monitored. At the end of the probationary period you will be informed in writing if you have successfully completed your probationary period.

If you have continuous employment this will be detailed on the cover page and counts towards your period of continuous employment with the Corporation.

Our formal Disciplinary and Performance Capability policies will not apply during your probationary period, but we will apply the principles of good practice to investigate any issue with you. If you commit an act of gross misconduct we reserve the right to dismiss you without notice.

Job Title and Details

You will be employed by the Corporation in the role as referenced in the covering table. You will report to your line manager (who is referenced in the covering table) or such other person as the Corporation may nominate from time to time.

You will be expected to perform duties consistent with your role as well as any additional or different duties as assigned to you by the Corporation from time to time.

Grievance and disciplinary procedures

You are subject to the Corporation's disciplinary and grievance procedures, copies of which are available from the Channel 4 Intranet. These procedures do not form part of your contract of employment.

The Corporation may suspend you from any or all of your duties for no longer than is necessary to investigate any disciplinary matter involving you or so long as is otherwise reasonable while any disciplinary procedure against you is outstanding. During any period of suspension you shall continue to receive your normal salary and benefits in the usual way, subject to the terms of any benefit arrangement and the Corporation will keep you informed of its investigations at regular intervals.

Working time

Your working hours and working pattern are set out on page 1 of this agreement.

The normal office week is 37 hours and office hours 09:30 – 18:00, Monday to Thursday; and 09:30 – 17:30 on Friday. You’re entitled to a one hour unpaid lunch break, which includes your statutory entitlement.

You will be required to work the hours as may be necessary for the proper performance of your duties.

You won’t, however, be required to work more than an average of 48 hours per week over 17 weeks. Additional payments won’t be made for time worked outside office hours.

Annual Leave

You’re entitled to [25] days' paid holiday in each holiday year [calculated on a pro rata basis to your start date and working hours together with the usual public holidays [or days in lieu where the Corporation requires you to work on a public holiday]. The Corporation’s holiday year runs between 1 April and 31 March [and is also the leave year for the purposes of parental leave]. Refer to the holiday policy on the Channel 4 Intranet.

Part‐Time or Job‐Share Working

If you work less than 37 hours per week, your holiday entitlement will be calculated on a pro‐rata basis.

Collective agreement

There is no Collective Agreement in force, which governs your terms and conditions of employment.

Termination of Employment

Your employment will continue until terminated by either you or the Corporation (“the parties”) giving to the other not less than [the notice indicated in the cover table on page 1] notice in writing.

The Corporation reserves the right, in its absolute discretion, to pay you your salary (excluding any variable pay and benefits) in lieu of any period of notice (less tax, National Insurance contributions and other deductions required by law).

Upon notice of termination, Channel 4 also reserves the right to place you on garden leave. This may mean that for some or all of the duration of your notice period, the Channel can require you to remain at home and you will have no contact or dealings with Channel 4 customers or suppliers and will not be able to take up employment with another employer.

Notwithstanding the other provisions of this agreement, the Corporation may, in its sole and absolute discretion, terminate your employment with immediate effect without notice and with no liability to make any further payment to you if you:

are guilty of gross misconduct; or

commit a material breach of the terms and conditions set out in this Agreement; or

are convicted of a criminal offence (other than a minor driving offence that does not prevent you from carrying out your duties); or

bring yourself or the Corporation into disrepute; or

cease to be eligible to work in the United Kingdom; or

are in breach of the Corporation’s anticorruption and bribery policy and related procedures.

Individuals on Fixed Term Contracts

Your contract covers a fixed term of employment but this term cannot be guaranteed as operational circumstances and/or requirement for your services may change. The Corporation therefore reserves the right to terminate your employment prior to the stated end date. In these circumstances the Corporation will terminate your appointment by giving you the notice period stated on page 1 of this agreement (or a payment in lieu of basic salary thereof).

Redundancy

In the unlikely event that your employment with the Corporation is terminated by reason of redundancy, you will be eligible to receive a statutory redundancy payment calculated in accordance with prevailing rules and legislation.

Please note that you will not be entitled to benefit from any discretionary policy in relation to enhanced redundancy payment, which the Corporation may have operated prior to your employment.

Accordingly, in redundancy situations, any additional payment over and above your entitlement to statutory redundancy pay will be entirely ex gratia and at the sole discretion of the Corporation.

BENEFITS
Flexible Benefits

You will become eligible to participate in the Channel 4 flexible benefits scheme (Flex) after three months service and satisfactory completion of your probationary period.

Please note that the FLEX scheme is provided at the entire discretion of the Corporation and benefits within the scheme may be changed or withdrawn from time to time, although reasonable warning will be given.

All Channel 4 benefits are subject to the rules of each scheme. The scale of cover and any applicable exclusion are at the discretion of the Corporation and are subject to change.

Channel 4 Pension Arrangements
The Defined Contribution Pension Scheme (“the Scheme”)

You will be eligible for inclusion in the Defined Contribution Scheme (Age‐related) offered through the Corporation after completion of three months continuous service following the date you join. An invitation will be automatically sent to you nearer the time so you can decide whether or not you wish to join the Scheme. In the meantime, more information about the Scheme is available on the Channel 4 intranet.

Life Assurance

You will be covered by our life assurance policy from your first day of employment. If you die whilst employed by us, a benefit of four times your current annual basic salary (up to a maximum benefit of £1.5 million per annum) will be payable subject to the terms and conditions of the policy. More information about our life assurance scheme can be found on the Channel 4 Intranet.

For maximum tax efficiency, the Corporation has absolute discretion in deciding to whom it is paid. It will, however, take into account your wishes when paying this lump sum, provided it has been notified beforehand. To do this, you should complete an Expression of Wish Form (remember to update it if your circumstances change).

Private Medical Insurance

You will, be covered by our group private medical insurance scheme, subject to the rules in force from time to time, after completion of three months continuous service. For administration purposes, the cover will commence from the first day of the following month after completion of this three‐month period. This is a taxable benefit – tax will be deducted from your salary on a monthly basis.

The scheme is reviewed each year and is subject to change. The Corporation reserves the right to change the scheme provider and the terms and conditions of the scheme.

Please note that you must tell us in writing if you don’t want to join the scheme. Further information can be found on the Channel 4 intranet.

Variable Pay Scheme

You are eligible to participate in a performance related variable pay scheme (as indicated on the cover page). Specific details regarding the scheme, including performance criteria is available on the Channel 4 intranet.

Any variable pay is entirely discretionary and there is no contractual entitlement to receive it, nor shall it be deemed to become part of the contractual remuneration or your salary for pension purposes or otherwise.

The Corporation reserves in its absolute discretion the right to terminate or amend the variable pay scheme of the Corporation without notice to you. Payment of performance related variable pay to you in any one year or other period shall not give you any right or legitimate expectation of any variable pay in respect of any subsequent year or other period.

Data Protection

You agree that any information which you provide about yourself to the Corporation and which the Corporation acquires about you from time to time during the course of the employment may be used and disclosed by the Corporation for administration and management purposes in relation to your employment. In particular, you consent to the Corporation processing Sensitive Personal Data (as defined in the Data Protection Act 1998) relating to you for such purposes, including as appropriate:

information about your physical or mental health condition in order to monitor sick leave and take decisions as to your fitness for work;

your racial or ethnic origin, religious or similar benefits and/or sexual orientation in order to monitor compliance with equal opportunities legislation;

information relating to any criminal proceedings in which you have been involved for insurance purposes and in order to comply with legal requirements and obligations to third parties; and information relating to your political opinions or union membership that may come into the knowledge of the Corporation (e.g. through any declaration of interests).

You agree to keep the Corporation informed of any changes to your personal data.

The Corporation may make such information available to any company, who provides products and services to the Corporation (such as advisors and payroll administrators), regulatory authorities and potential or future employers.

Where you provide details of any other individual to the Corporation (e.g. your next of kin for ‘in case of emergency’ purposes) you warrant that you have received the consent of the individual for the Corporation to hold this information.

You agree that you may, in the course of your employment, have access to personal data concerning individuals who access the Corporation’s website, or other personal data. You are required to treat such information in the strictest confidence, and to take all steps as may be specified by the Corporation to prevent the unauthorised disclosure of such data or any processing of it which would be contrary to the provisions of the Data Protection Act 1998 (as amended or superseded from time to time). Failure by you to take any such steps as have been specified in this regard by the Corporation or any unauthorised disclosure or processing of personal data shall be regarded as gross misconduct.

You understand that the Corporation may be obliged to disclose certain information about you (for example your name and salary range, etc.) pursuant to a request under the Freedom of Information Act 2000 or other regulatory or statutory requirements.

RESTRICTIONS DURING YOUR EMPLOYMENT
Competitive activity

During your employment with us you’ll devote your whole working time and attention to Channel 4’s business.

Prior written consent will be required to engage in business activities outside of your employment. Requests must be made in writing to your Head of Department.

Generally, there is no restriction placed on private occupations or activities, provided that your activities do not compete directly with the Corporation and occur outside normal working hours. Working for a competitor of the Corporation is strictly prohibited at any time whilst you are employed by the Corporation and may lead to disciplinary action being taken against you.

Should consent be given to engage in business activities outside of your employment agreement, activities should not be carried out in normal working hours when you would be required to be available for work under your employment agreement; nor should such activities make use of Channel 4 information or experience acquired in the course of Channel 4 business. You must also ensure that your work doesn’t suffer and that you are not working in excess of an average of 48 hours per week over a 17 week period.

Confidentiality

While you are with us, you will have access to and be entrusted with key information relating to our business. This information is confidential and must be kept secret at all times.

You must not use or divulge to a third party, other than for the benefit of the Corporation (and in the course of carrying out your duties), any confidential or sensitive information relating to our business. In addition, you should try to prevent the disclosure of such information by other persons.

All documents, data or software relating to any aspect of the Corporation’s activities remain the property of the Corporation and at no time should you use them or permit them to be used other than for the Corporation’s benefit. Similarly, you shouldn’t at any time copy, abstract or summarise any part of documents, data or software that belong to the Corporation.

The above obligations continue after you leave us. It is therefore essential that before leaving, you return any documents, data or software, which you obtained whilst employed by us.

Code of Conduct

You are subject to the Corporation's Code of Conduct, which can be found on the Channel 4 Intranet. However, this does not form part of your contract of employment.

Everything we do, everything we say, and everything our key stakeholders say about us defines Channel 4’s reputation. Our stakeholders are identified as our employees, viewers/users, customers, governments, the media, our communities, and commercial partners – including suppliers and customers.

Each of us has regular contact with one or more of these stakeholder groups in undertaking our daily work. It is important that in doing so our communication guidelines are followed and the advice of the Press Office and Corporate Relations teams is sought where appropriate.

Our day‐to‐day relations with the media are managed by the Press Office.

Management of our day‐to‐day relations with the government, its departments, and parliament is handled by our Corporate Relations team.

Specifically, you should:
  • Direct all media to the Press Office
  • Avoid speaking to the media unless specifically authorised to do so
  • Avoid acting as a spokesperson for Channel 4, its people, brands or performance, without first consulting a member of the Press Office
  • Ensure all media announcements and press releases are approved in advance by an appropriate member of the press office.”
LEAVING US: RESTRICTIONS AFTER EMPLOYMENT
Non‐disclosure

When your employment with us ends, you must not discuss with or disclose to any person, firm or company, whether in public or in private, orally or in writing, any confidential information about Channel 4. This includes information relating to our business, finances, programming or other internal affairs.

This restriction shall cease to apply to information that has become public knowledge (other than by your default), or to any disclosure of such information required by law or by order of any Court, tribunal or similar regulatory body.

Derogatory or pejorative statements

During your employment with us and after it ends, you mustn’t make any derogatory or pejorative statement to any person, firm or company, of any kind about Channel 4 or individual employees. This applies whether in public or in private, orally or in writing.

Non‐solicitation

When your employment with us ends, you must not for a period of six months from your last day of employment solicit or induce to breach his/her contract of employment or contract for services, or entice away from Channel 4 any Key Person in our employment (Key Person means any employee who is engaged by Channel 4 in a senior capacity and with whom you had material dealings during the 12 month period prior to the end of your employment). Upon leaving Channel 4, you must also not for a period of 6 months from your last day of employment employ, engage or procure the employment or engagement in any capacity by you or by any person, firm, corporation or organisation of any Key Person at Channel 4. Any breach of this term may lead to Channel 4 taking legal action against you.
