W R Systems, Ltd. Terms and Conditions of Employment

Employee acknowledges that, due to the nature of his or her relationship with W R Systems, Ltd. (WR), he or she will have access to confidential and proprietary information, including trade secrets, relating to the business and operations of WR, and that disclosure of such information to, or its use by, others could cause substantial harm or loss to WR;

In consideration of his or her employment by WR, Employee acknowledges and agrees as follows:

1.
Definitions. For the purposes of this Agreement:
A.
“WR Proprietary Information” means any information, data, computer software, computer software in source code form, invention, design, idea, concept, specification, formula, device, equipment, plan, process, technical information concerning manufacturing or operating processes, equipment design, product specifications, document or material whether tangible or intangible (including, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, information related to marketing strategies or plans, business strategies or plans, selling and pricing information and procedures, pricing policies or plans, proposals, lists of customers or clients, internal financial statements, and any other data or information which, in any way whatsoever, pertains to marketing or sales), which is a trade secret or proprietary in nature, which is not generally known to outsiders or WR’s competitors and which (i) belongs to or is in the possession of WR or any client or customer of WR or (ii) is learned or developed, in whole or in part, by Employee or otherwise comes into Employee’s possession, control, or knowledge in connection with or arising out of Employee’s employment by WR.
B.
“Employee Work Product” means any idea, intellectual property right, invention, discovery, development, design, technique, improvement plan, work of authorship, software, programming, documentation, technology, data information, enhancement, or other work product, whether tangible or intangible, which is (i) in whole or in part conceived, made, developed, reduced to practice, prepared, or otherwise created by Employee, whether within or outside Employee’s normal hours of employment, while employed by WR and (ii) related to, connected with, or arising out of the business or activities of WR.
2.
Employment At Will. The employment of Employee by WR is an “At Will” relationship and may be ended by either party at any time, either with or without cause. Immediately upon the end of Employee’s employment with WR, Employee shall deliver to WR all tangible assets or information, regardless of form or medium, in the possession or control of Employee, which relate to, are connected with, or arise out of Employee’s employment with WR, including, but not limited to, all WR Proprietary Information and all Employee Work Product.
3.
Duty of Loyalty and Conflicts of Interest. Employee acknowledges that he or she owes a duty of loyalty to WR, and while employed by WR, will use his or her best efforts to serve the interests of WR. While employed by WR, Employee shall not discuss with a client of WR the availability of services from a business, person, or other entity that is or will be in competition with WR.

Employee further acknowledges an obligation to avoid any conflict of interest. A conflict of interest exists if any outside business or other interests may adversely affect Employee’s motivation or performance (including, but not limited to, whether Employee is actually improperly influenced and whether the situation lends itself to improperly influencing Employee). Employee may not serve as a consultant to or as a director, officer, employee, or significant shareholder (i.e. more than 5% interest) of an entity that competes or deals with WR or that seeks to do so, unless Employee has obtained the prior written consent of WR. Employee further acknowledges a duty to report any known conflicts of interest within WR.

4.
Non-Disclosure of Proprietary Information. WR has a great deal of valuable, confidential, and proprietary information relating to its business, which if disclosed to or used by a competitor, would cause WR irreparable injury. WR will disclose this information to Employee in reliance on Employee’s promise made herein not to misuse the information. At all times while employed by WR and at all times thereafter:
 
A.
Employee agrees to treat all WR Proprietary Information in a secret and confidential manner and shall comply with all applicable procedures established by WR with respect to maintaining the secrecy and confidentiality of WR Proprietary Information;
B.
Employee agrees to treat all WR Proprietary Information only as necessary and proper in the performance of Employee’s duties as an employee of WR, solely for WR’s internal business purposes and not as a basis for personal gain by Employee;
C.
Except as required above or by law, Employee agrees to not directly or indirectly, without the prior written consent of WR, reproduce, copy, disseminate, publish, disclose, provide, or otherwise make available to any person, firm, corporation, agency, or other entity (including family, friends, associates, or other employees of WR who do not require such information to discharge their duties) any WR Proprietary Information;
D.
Employee agrees to provide such reasonable assistance as may be requested by WR in the effort to maintain the secrecy and confidentiality of WR Proprietary Information; and,
C.
If Employee had access to a previous employer’s trade secret information or proprietary information, Employee agrees to not use this information while an employee of WR unless the prior employer first gives written permission for use of such information as intended by Employee.
5.
Employee Work Product. Employee agrees to render all workmanlike services and to promptly and fully disclose to his or her immediate supervisor all Employee Work Product. All Employee Work Product shall be conclusively deemed to be conceived, made, developed, reduced to practice, prepared, or otherwise created within the scope of Employee’s employment and shall, as between WR and Employee, be the sole property of WRS. Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to WR all rights, titles, and interest of whatever nature which Employee may have in and to Employee Work Product. Such obligations shall be binding upon Employee’s assigns, executors, administrators, and other legal representatives.
6.
Employee Policies. Employee acknowledges that he or she will adhere to the general employment policies and procedures of the WR Employee Policy Manual.
7.
Remedies for Breach. Employee acknowledges and understands that a breach of any of the provisions contained herein may result in continuing and irreparable damages to WR for which there may be no adequate remedy at law and that WR in addition to all other relief available to WR shall be entitled to the issuance of an injunction restraining Employee from committing or containing any breach of this Agreement.
8.
Enforcement of Agreement. If either party at any time fails to enforce any provision of this Agreement, it shall not constitute a waiver of such provision in any way or prevent such party at any time from taking advantage of any remedies as it may have for any breach of such provision.
9.
Governing Laws and Forum. This Agreement shall be governed by, construed, and enforced in accordance with the laws of the Commonwealth of Virginia. The parties hereto further agree that any action brought to enforce any right or obligation under this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Virginia.
10.
Amendments and Termination. This Agreement may not be amended or terminated except by a writing executed by both Employee and an authorized officer of WR. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between WR and Employee relating to this subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior oral and written understandings and agreements relating to such subject matter unless expressly incorporated herein. If any provision of this Agreement is judicially determined to be invalid or unenforceable as written, then such provision shall, if possible, be modified and reformed to the degree necessary to render it valid and enforceable. Any such invalidity of any provision shall have no effect on the remainder of this Agreement, which shall remain in full force and effect.
11.
Acknowledgment. Employee acknowledges that he or she has read the above Agreement in its entirety, has had an opportunity to seek legal counsel, and understands and agrees to each provision of this Agreement.
